# War Pigs (Black Sabbath) + Three Little Pigs (Traditional)

__[INSTRUMENTAL START]__

*Once upon a time, in Shanklin, there lived an old sow, an old mother pig, with her litter of three little pigs... But with another litter on the way — wasn't it always the same? — there was no room for them any more. So she had to send them on their way, with a warning about not necessarily trusting everyone you meet on the road. The word got out, and a wolf that happened to live around about those parts heard that some little pigs might be hitting the road... and if you're a wolf, well, a nice bit of pork would make a welcome change from chicken or lamb...*

__[DUH DUHH]__

Generals gathered in their masses  
Just like witches at black masses  
Evil minds that plot destruction  
Sorcerer of death's construction

In the fields, the bodies burning  
As the war machine keeps turning  
Death and hatred to mankind  
Poisoning their brainwashed minds  
Oh, Lord, yeah

*The first little pig, with an excited step in his trot, set off, and quite quickly along the way met a man pushing a barrow load of straw. etc. Built house, "little pig", "huff, puff", eaten...*

*The second little pig didn't really want to go — I don't know if he knew what had happened to his brother or not — but leave he must, and as that little pig left the sty, taking one last, longing, look behind him, his mother wiped a tear from her eye...*

*Now, the second little pig wasn't far along the way, when he saw a man with a cart load of furze etc. Built house, "little pig", "huff, puff", eaten...*

Politicians hide themselves away  
They only started the war  
Why should they go out to fight?  
They leave that role to the poor, yeah

*It was time for the third little pig to leave home. Now for this little pig, he'd always had to be quick witted, what with having two brothers, who could be as thick as thieves against you at times... So when __he__ left home, he told his mother not to worry, because he knew how to look after himself...*

*As the third little pig went on his way, he saw a man coming with a pile of bricks (there was perhaps a landfill tax even in those days...). Built house, "little pig", "huff, puff", but could not blow the house down, so with cunning an guile, he suggested that they go together, early in the morning, to a nearby turnip field... But the third little pig also had a plan...*

Time will tell on their power minds  
Making war just for fun  
Treating people just like pawns in chess  
Wait 'til their judgement day comes, yeah

`[Story goes long for a bit..]`

*Went early, tricked wolf. Wolf had another idea, apples. But the same thing happens... So the wolf has a third plan, market, pig rolls in barrel, scares wolf...*

Now in darkness, world stops turning  
Ashes where their bodies burning  
No more war pigs have the power  
Hand of God has struck the hour

*The wolf was furious, went to the house, inside a pot was boiling, wolf went on to roof, down chimney, and into the pot...*

Day of judgement, God is calling  
On their knees, the war pigs crawling  
Begging mercy for their sins  
Satan laughing, spreads his wings  
Oh, Lord, yeah

*The wolf screamed for mercy, but the third little pig would have none of it; and he ate well that day...*

*And such is the tale of the Three Little Pigs, a tale that was originally collected from Arreton in the 1840s or so, and that made it's way into one of the first collections of English Nursery tTales and Nursery Rhymes... And it just goes to show, the stories — and storytelling — even of the traditional tales, is not just about reading books to kids...*
